## Why this article exists

Ra teaches inside a living lineage and openly names his own teachers. The lineage shapes his authority, his teaching style, and the *way* he positions himself — as a mid-level master connecting students to higher-degree teachers, not as the top of the hierarchy. This article collects who those teachers are and how Ra relates to them.

## Structural overview

Ra's lineage has two streams that converge in him:

1. **Inherited / familial stream** — his parents' healing tradition, rooted in India.
2. **Self-found guru stream** — three successive gurus, each operating at higher degrees of mastery, each advancing Ra's realization.

The family stream is the *healing* pathway; the guru stream is the *enlightenment* pathway. Ra chose the latter but was enabled by the former.

## 1. Familial stream — parents and Vedic ancestry

Ra's parents were Energy Healers in India. From satsang:

> *"Both my parents were considered gurus... my father especially, thousands of people... even though he was not a guru in terms of somebody who brings you to enlightenment... His focus was on healing."*

Ra has also spoken publicly about his paternal Vedic-rishi ancestry:

> *"Singing Saraswati mantras, because we are actually Sarasvata Brahmins, we used to live on the riverbank of Saraswati, our ancestors, and that's where the Vedas were written, and my paternal lineage goes back to one of the sages who wrote the Vedas in the Saraswati riverbank."*

This places Ra's paternal line in the rishi tradition of the Sarasvati riverbank — one of the canonical Vedic origin sites. It's part of why Ra's fluency across Sanskrit, Hindu cosmology, and Guru Parampara vocabulary is inherited scholarly identity, not only acquired through his three gurus.

## 2. Pre-guru catalyzing event — Auroville

> *"A particular healing that happened to me when I was ready... just before I met my first Guru... at Sri Aurobindo's place, Auroville, in India."*

Sri Aurobindo is not a direct teacher in Ra's chain, but the *location* of a pre-guru healing that opened the way to the first teacher. Ra speaks of Auroville with reverence.

## 3. Guru stream — three teachers

### First guru (unnamed)

Ra's first guru brought him to his first enlightenment — his **first-degree black belt** in Ra's own metaphor. This teacher's lineage traces to **Ramana Maharshi**. Ra stayed in Tiruvannamalai at Ramana's ashram and the sacred mountain there because his first teacher came annually and said the mountain's energy was the reason.

Ra has chosen not to publicly name this teacher.

Parting instruction from the first guru, which Ra still quotes as emblematic of the register:

> *"Watch the star shine."*

### Ahjan — second guru, 10th-degree master

Ahjan was a Buddhist mystic from whom Ra received his 2nd-, 3rd-, and 4th-degree realizations, compressed into roughly one month of accelerated transmission during a period when Ahjan himself was dealing with cancer.

From satsang:

> *"So when I came into Ahjan school, Ahjan was like a 10th degree black belt, but from my previous teacher, I had already received my first enlightenment."*

Ahjan explicitly asked Ra to teach. That is when Ra's public teaching mission was lit.

Ahjan was known for **public, wrathful corrections** — a pedagogical move that directly informs how Ra receives corrections today and shapes the teaching [Correction as Blessing](../teachings/correction-as-blessing.md):

> *"I used to receive very rarely corrections from Ahjan, but when I did, it would be very wrathful, very wrathful, oh my god, he would go all in, he would correct people in front of the entire community."*

Ahjan healed from the cancer and has since withdrawn from active teaching.

### Sai Maa — current guru, 10+ degree master

From satsang:

> *"So Saima, she's much more than 10th degree black belt... she's been teaching for 25 years. Teaching is also a way to deepen enlightenment."*

> *"Her guru was... an avatar of a great magnitude."*

**Sai Maa's lineage** traces to **Shirdi Sai Baba** (her root lineage guru, through Satya Sai Baba's parampara). **Mahavatar Babaji** is also named in her lineage, particularly as the teacher who gave her ascension work.

Ra is actively, currently studying with Sai Maa. He has attended her **Journey of Profound Healing** program and continues to learn from her. She is an active teacher for him — he is simultaneously a teacher to his mentees and a student of hers.

## How Ra positions himself within this chain

Ra is unusually transparent about being at a *specific middle level* of the hierarchy:

> *"Very powerful to have a fourth, fifth kind of degree black belt to teach you how to get your first degree black belt while also being in the lineage of multiple black belts, multiple enlightened masters."*

The pedagogical argument: a student aiming for their first degree is better served by a 4th- or 5th-degree teacher who is *close* to the beginner's path — and who is also connected upward to higher-degree teachers the student can eventually move toward — than by a 10th-degree master teaching at altitude. This framing is one of Ra's key defenses against spiritual-teacher egoism. He cannot claim to be the highest, and he says so directly.

## Other teachers Ra references

Appreciatively but not as direct teachers in his chain:

- **Ramana Maharshi** — realized teacher; location of Ra's first-teacher's work
- **Ajahn Chah** — cited for childlike joy in an enlightened teacher
- **Sri Aurobindo** — location-of-healing reference, not direct teacher
- **Mahavatar Babaji** — lineage ancestor through Sai Maa

## Traditions Ra draws from

Ra's language crosses several traditions fluently without claiming exclusive identity with any:

- **Buddhism** (Theravada: Satipatthana, klesha, lojong; Mahayana: bodhicitta; Vajrayana: his own ordination)
- **Hindu / Tantric** (guru, diksha, kundalini, chakras, samskara, Guru Parampara)
- **Advaita** (*neti neti*, non-dual pointing, *"there is no other people"*)
- **Christian mysticism** (in anecdotal reference — e.g., his mother's prayer *"to give this child to God"*)
- **His own idiosyncratic pedagogy** (martial-arts black-belt framework, 108-post metaphor, hooks-and-loops)

The mixing is intentional. Ra does not teach *as a Buddhist* or *as a Hindu* — he teaches as a realized being who uses whichever tradition's language lands for a given seeker.
