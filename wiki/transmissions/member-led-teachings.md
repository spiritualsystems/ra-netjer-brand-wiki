## What it is

A **recurring pattern** in the I AM Community: long-term AWR members sometimes lead short teaching series inside Ra's container — bringing their own experience, area of practice, or complementary modality into a community session, hosted in the field Ra holds.

This is a distinctive feature of the community's culture: the teaching is not only one-to-many from Ra to students. Members with depth in specific areas (meditation traditions, healing modalities, specific life-practice arcs) occasionally hold sessions. Ra curates and supports, rather than delegating wholesale.

## Why this pattern exists

Several structural reasons:

1. **Sangha-as-accelerator** (see [Three Jewels](../teachings/three-jewels-of-buddhism.md)) — members teaching members is a direct expression of the inner-Sangha teaching. The community is not only a support structure; it is a transmission field in which members' realization can serve others.
2. **Complementary modalities.** Ra's teaching is enlightenment-focused. Some members carry practice depth in specific domains (breathwork, body practice, healing, creative process) that complement the core transmission.
3. **Acceleration-of-normalcy.** The aspiration that enlightenment become *commonplace* ([enlightenment as new normal](../teachings/enlightenment-as-new-normal.md)) implies more than one teacher's output. The community rhythm compounds when multiple voices carry the work.

## What these sessions are not

- Not a replacement for Ra's teaching — member-led sessions sit alongside Ra's weekly anchor sessions, not in place of them
- Not a credential-conferring path — a member-led teaching is not a promotion to teacher status
- Not commercial — these are community sessions, not standalone offerings

## How to participate

Member-led sessions appear on the community's weekly schedule alongside Ra's sessions. They are accessible through the same community pathway as other I AM Community offerings.
