## What retreats are in Ra's practice

A **distinct transmission format** from the weekly satsangs and weekly mentorship. Retreats combine:

- **Place of power** (see [../teachings/places-of-power.md](../teachings/places-of-power.md)) — pilgrimage to sacred geography
- **Time of power** — equinoxes, Navaratri, Ra's birthday, lineage holy days
- **Multi-day immersion** — several days continuous; not a single event
- **In-person core + online satellite** — small physical cohort plus online attendees joining via a live selfie-stick video feed
- **Transmission amplification** — the retreat field intensifies what the weekly anchor teachings deliver

Retreats serve the Integrated Awakening Program by giving students accelerated, concentrated access to Ra's teaching in a field where places of power and community presence compound the transmission.

## Two format modalities

### Adventure retreat (outdoor, places of power, flexible schedule)

- No fixed schedule; Ra leads by attunement to the day and the field
- Outdoor components: canyon night-sits, desert meditation, hot springs, volcano and lava-field visits, ocean / mountain encounters
- Temple visits for lineage connection
- Shared silent meals (monastic meals where available)
- Public "demonstrations" of advanced practice — what Ra's second guru called *"Buddhist magic"*
- **Selfie-stick online format** — Ra carries a phone on a selfie stick during walks so online attendees can join the field live

### Relaxation / integration retreat

Smaller format. Features: sauna, lazy river, hot tub — integration and rest rather than intensity. Used between heavier adventure formats.

## What retreats do that other formats don't

1. **Field intensification** — the small cohort + place of power produce transmissions the weekly anchor teachings cannot match.
2. **Lineage geography** — retreats put students in the actual geographic lineage (sites where Ra's own teachers and their teachers did their work). Not reproducible online.
3. **Integration time** — multi-day continuity lets an opening deepen rather than fragment.
4. **Cohort bonding** — attendees refer back to shared retreat moments for years afterward. The cohort becomes a lasting transmission node.
