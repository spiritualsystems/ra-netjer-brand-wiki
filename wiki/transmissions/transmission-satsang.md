## What it is

Ra's **Transmission Teaching & Satsang** is a weekly, multi-hour live gathering combining teaching, energetic transmission, and open attendee sharing. Scheduled on Sundays; hybrid format (in-person with Ra when he is traveling; others join online via Zoom).

On the website, this offering is implicit inside the Free I AM Community benefit list (*"Free Weekly Meditations on Zoom,"* *"Live Zoom Classes,"* *"Years of powerful transmission teachings"*). In practice it is one of the core weekly rhythms of the community.

## Structure

| Phase | Approx. duration | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening / gathering** | ~10–15 min | Community moderator welcomes attendees by name; guides collective receptivity invocation; acknowledges Ra's arrival |
| **Pre-teaching conversation** | ~10 min | Informal; Ra shares recent events (e.g., his travel, his continuing studentship with his own guru) |
| **Core teaching** | ~25–45 min | Ra teaches a central concept with embedded transmission throughout |
| **Attendee sharing / integration** | ~60–90 min | Attendees raise hands to share experiences, breakthroughs, questions. Ra responds to each with teaching + direct energetic address |
| **Closing** | ~5–10 min | Ra closes with a poetic invocation; collective mantra honoring the lineage |

## Teaching character — different from mentorship

Ra's satsang voice differs from his mentorship voice:

- **Metaphor-sustained.** Ra extends a single metaphor (martial-arts belts, the unknown, the hall of mirrors, the mile-post) across 30+ minutes as the central teaching arc.
- **Lineage-acknowledging.** Satsangs reference Ra's gurus and the broader lineage more openly than mentorship sessions. Satsang is where Ra is most explicit about his own studentship.
- **Collective field.** Ra's attention is distributed across the room; the energetic transmission addresses the group as a whole before addressing individuals.
- **Poetic closings.** Satsangs end with invocation-language (infinity, unfoldment, the mystery). Mentorship calls end more practically.

## Cultural rituals

- **Opening invocation** (by moderator): *"Let's all enter into a receptive state of being... Surrender all your littleness, all your filters and labels... Find that place of peace in your heart. Open up your heart. Open up your mind."*
- **Greeting Ra's arrival:** *"Welcome, Ra, whatever you're getting. Namaste."*
- **Multiple-Namaste satsang opening** — Ra himself opens extended satsangs with a ritualized *"Namaste. Namaste. Namaste. Namaste. Namaste. Ah. Hmm. Hmm. Hmm. Wow. Hmm. Hmm. Good to see you all. Today is such a powerful day."*
- **Collective mantra:** a lineage-invoking mantra used multiple times throughout the evening
- **Call-and-response energy:** Ra says *"Very good," "Wonderful," "Wow"* frequently, validating speakers

## Role in the offering stack

- **Entry point.** For a new seeker, satsang is often the first direct exposure to Ra. Attended freely via the I AM Community.
- **Continuing rhythm for program students.** Even after a seeker enters the paid Integrated Awakening Program, satsangs continue — the program is additive, not a replacement.
- **Cross-lineage gathering.** Satsangs are where Ra's students and students from adjacent lineage communities mix; the lineage connection is lived rather than theoretical.

## Why this offering is structurally important

Three structural features make satsang load-bearing for the whole practice:

1. **It is where lineage is publicly made visible.** Ra names his teachers, honors them, invokes them. This is the main offering where seekers see Ra inside a bigger tradition — not as a standalone teacher.
2. **It is where mutual-witness is enacted.** When attendees share breakthroughs in front of the collective, transformation becomes communal rather than private. *"Each and every one of you transform... opening for everybody else to transform. This is how we accelerate all together."*
3. **It is where the teaching arc appears.** Mentorship is responsive (addressing whatever a student is stuck on). Satsang is more structured — a whole teaching is transmitted with internal coherence. Most of the [teachings/](../teachings/) articles in this wiki are seeded from satsang material.
