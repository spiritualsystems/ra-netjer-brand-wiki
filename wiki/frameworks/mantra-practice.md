## Headline

**Ra's step-by-step public methodology for mantra practice**, from the 1h17m public YouTube teaching "Exact, simple method to Chant Mantras to find GOD within!" (2025-10-27). The most complete public articulation of Ra's mantra-practice framework. Uses Om Namah Shivaya as the teaching vehicle while noting the pattern generalizes to any mantra.

## Etymology as foundation — "Man-tra"

- **Man** = mind. Source of bondage and potential instrument of liberation.
- **Tra** = *traim* = save me, savior.
- **Mantra therefore means**: savior of the mind, or using the mind correctly to move from insignificant-ego-identification to eternal-self recognition.

Cross-tradition equivalents Ra explicitly equalizes: Islamic *La ilaha illallah*, Jewish *Ein od milvado*, Christian unceasing prayer (Desert Fathers tradition), Hare Krishna. All follow the same mechanism.

## Core anatomy — Om Namah Shivaya

Ra uses Om Namah Shivaya as the universal teaching model, with "Shiva" as an explicit placeholder for any deity-name:

- **Om** = the primordial vibration; God, eternity, higher self. *"In the beginning was the word and the word was God."*
- **Namaha** = *not me, not mine*. Na = not; Ma = me/mine. The antidote to the ego's constant mantra of "I, me, mine."
- **Shivaya** = *to your true nature*. Shiva = auspicious (Sanskrit). The deity-name is a personification placeholder for the seeker's own higher self.

**Full translation**: *"Om — invoking the primordial; Namaha — not me, not mine; Shivaya — to my true nature."*

> *"When the mind is completely absorbed in this practice... the mantra finally disappears. There is no mantra also. You go beyond the mantra. And all that remains is silence, stillness."*

## Why vibration precedes understanding

Mantras carry power even without intellectual understanding. The vibration itself activates corresponding dimensions of the chakra system.

> *"Even if you don't understand them, they have power. Your being, your soul, remembers something."*

Understanding and heart-engagement add power, but the vibrational channel is operational at the level of the soul's remembrance, not the mind's comprehension.

## Common mistakes

1. **Quantity over quality.** Counting 108 repetitions on a mala as the goal misses the mechanism. *"Focus on quality of your chanting — it makes all the difference."*
2. **Chanting without understanding.** People learn translations from scholars who lack direct experience.
3. **Inconsistency / wrong timing.** Missing the leverage point of morning practice before the ego establishes its momentum.

## Step-by-step practice

### Phase 1 — Pre-ritual (reverence)

Set a clear intention. Optional: light incense, sit at an altar, do whatever creates a sense of honoring the practice.

### Phase 2 — Morning entry point

Begin as close to waking as possible, before the ego establishes its momentum. *"As soon as your mind becomes aware of even existing in the morning, that is when the thoughts are just beginning. That is the right time."*

### Phase 3 — Quality-depth chanting

Bring both awareness/understanding and heart simultaneously. Chant slowly — *"just once per breath,"* with silence between each repetition for contemplation. Bring the deeper meaning into the heart, not just the head.

> *"Don't chant it fast... even one word per breath — because what happens is, time becomes an illusion."*

### Phase 4 — The dissolution point

Signs it's working: altered sense of time, body-awareness dissolving, experiential sense of unity with God.

> *"When the mantra stops naturally and complete absorption takes over — do not restart the mantra. You've climbed to the top. So why do you need the ladder? It's time to discard the ladder, the mantra. All that remains is silence, stillness, peace, complete peace."*

### Phase 5 — Nity sadhana (continuous practice)

Once started in the morning, the mantra runs continuously as a background practice. Nitya (Sanskrit: *eternal*, often mistranslated as *daily*) means continuous, not periodic.

> *"You stray, you come back. No matter how many times you stray from the mantra, the only thing you need to do is come back."*

## The role of initiation

> *"When I initiate my students, I am giving it as a transmission teaching. I am empowering the mantra and I'm transmitting my awareness, my deeper seeing of the truth of that mantra that I have experienced in my deepest meditations."*

The initiation includes:
- Transmission of the teacher's direct realization of the mantra
- Energetic empowerment in the practitioner's field
- Deeper meaning beyond dictionary translation

## Relationship to bhakti

> *"There is a whole aspect of mantra chanting which is the devotion, the heart aspect of it, which a lot of people miss... If you're chanting the mantra with only your head, it doesn't really work. You have to bring your heart into it."*

The deity-name in the mantra is the *bhakti door* — the personification of God that makes devotion possible before the seeker is ready for pure non-dual practice.
