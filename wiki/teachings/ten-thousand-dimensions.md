## The teaching in one line

Enlightenment is not a single state to be reached — it is a multidimensional unfolding with at least 10,000 distinct dimensions of your true nature, each of which can be directly experienced, not merely conceptualized.

## Attribution

Ra credits this framing to his second guru (the Buddhist mystic, Ahjan):

> *"My second teacher called this 10,000 dimensions of enlightenment. Actually, there are more, but 10,000 seems like a big enough word."*

Source: 2026-01-05 YouTube transmission teaching satsang.

## The core mechanics

- Each time a practitioner enters a samadhi, a deep meditation, or a high state of consciousness, they are accessing **a different dimension of their true nature of enlightenment**.
- Every experience of this kind changes the person. They return to their ordinary human self but the self is *slightly more free*, *slightly lighter*, with *a little more ease*.
- The process is **cumulative and repeatable**: doing this again and again accelerates the recognition that one is not limited to the small human self.
- The transmission teaching context (satsang) is the vehicle Ra uses to help practitioners enter these dimensions. He explicitly contrasts *experiencing* a dimension with *conceptually understanding* it.

## What "dimensions" means here

Not science-fiction parallel universes. The term refers to **distinct aspects or facets of one's own unlimited nature** — analogous to how a diamond has many facets, each revealing a different quality of the same gem.

Examples from the satsang's sharing period:

- Seeing an aura with eyes open for the first time — a new visual dimension of perception
- Awareness dwelling within while simultaneously engaging the external
- A somatic/energetic dimension of purification as the heart opens
- Stillness and third-eye tingling despite a strong mind
- Colored lights and cosmic dance felt as one's own nature
- The spontaneous arising of the voice "I am enlightenment"

The variance across experiencers confirms Ra's framing that each practitioner encounters "exactly what they need for their next step."

## The community function

Ra explains that sharing experiences after the transmission teaching serves two purposes:

1. **Solidifies the experience in the experiencer**: speaking it aloud engages the part of the mind that might dismiss it as hallucination.
2. **Extends the experience to others**: hearing someone describe a dimension opens that dimension for others — a collective field amplification.

This is the structural rationale for the satsang sharing-circle: it is not social sharing but a continuation of the transmission.

## The normalcy aspiration

> *"I want enlightenment to be the new normal in your life."*

The ambition is not one profound experience once in a lifetime, but weekly — even daily — access to these dimensions until they stop being extraordinary.

## Key phrase

> *"Going into 10,000 dimensions of enlightenment."*
