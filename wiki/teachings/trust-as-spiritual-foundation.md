## The teaching in one line

> *"If you can develop one thing, I would say develop trust."*

Trust is Ra's answer to "what is the single most important quality on the spiritual path?" — ahead of practice, knowledge, or technique. It is the disposition that sustains all other practices, especially through the Dark Night. Sourced from a 34-minute public YouTube monologue, 2025-02-13, "If you have this, everything else will come!"

## What trust is — and what it is not

Ra begins with a definition problem: words like *trust*, *faith*, *surrender*, *love* are each received differently. The word's resonance matters more than its dictionary entry.

> *"Trust is so much more than believing in something, having faith in something... what I connected to as Trust was this feeling, this deep confidence, it goes beyond confidence, this deep loving sense of okayness, that everything is okay right now and everything will be okay."*

Key distinction: trust is not placed in something **external** — not in a captain, not in a circumstance, not even in a teacher. It is placed in the larger intelligence that moves everything, which is not separate from the seeker:

> *"What I am trusting in is not separate from me. It's not something that is a faraway man in the clouds looking down upon me."*

## Trust as ego-subsidence, not ego-endorsement

Ra invokes the Sanskrit verse *Tena vina trinamapi na calati* — "Without the will of God, not even a blade of grass moves." He explicitly blocks the ego's trap ("my will is therefore the will of God"):

> *"If it is taken by the ego, it can be twisted into another tool for the ego. But that is not the intention of these teachings. This is to strengthen your connection to your higher self, which means overcome the ego."*

Trust is the mechanism of ego-subsidence, not a cognitive belief about God.

## Trust through the Dark Night

> *"Through this dark night of the soul, trust was the little guiding lamp in that darkness. That is what kept me going even when my ego said there is absolutely no reason to live anymore."*

The phrase **"little guiding lamp in the darkness"** is Ra's most compressed articulation of trust's role in DNOS. It is neither a blinding solution nor an erasure of pain — it is small, consistent light.

Ra's micro-practice: *"I would say, I trust life. I would take a deep breath. I would connect."* Three moves — name, breathe, reconnect.

## Two anchors Ra names

**The Bhagavad Gita on time:**
> *"All that happened has happened well. All that is happening is happening well. All that will happen will also happen well."*

**Emerson on tragedy as gift:** *"Hidden within every tragedy is a seed of a much greater blessing."* Ra extends:
> *"Life is sometimes a really shitty gift wrapper... There's no bow tie on top, and it's not in red shiny paper, but there's a gift there nonetheless."*

## The causal chain

1. **Trust** (okayness that life is intelligent) →
2. **Acceptance** (of what is, without judgment that it should be otherwise) →
3. **Opening** (the gift is received because the seeker stopped running from the wrapper)

## The ego-mimicking-intuition caution

Trust in life's intelligence is not trust in everything that arises in consciousness. The ego can mimic intuition and disguise itself as divine guidance:

> *"Before you believe, before you trust your thoughts and give your power away to them, understand that just because they appear in your awareness, doesn't mean they're real."*

## Trust as the root of other virtues

> *"Trust also helps you develop other things, which are known as virtues on the path: humility, patience, love. It brings peace. It brings joy."*

You cannot reliably cultivate humility, patience, or love directly. But trust in the larger intelligence removes the fear-contractions that block them.
