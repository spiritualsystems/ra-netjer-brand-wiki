## The teaching

External stories (about your life, others, yourself) have **hooks** that stick. But they cannot stick to nothing — they stick to **loops** inside your psyche. Every hook needs a loop. The loop is a fear, a desire, a self-definition, or some other internal structure that the story can latch onto. Without the internal loop, the external story just passes through.

The practical implication: **stop working on the story. Work on the loop.** You cannot clear every story that will ever come at you. You *can* dissolve the internal loops, and when you do, nothing sticks anymore.

The metaphor Ra uses: **Velcro**. Hooks on one side, loops on the other. If either side is missing, nothing holds.

## Stated directly

> *"Stories have a hook, but they need a place within you to hook into... Each story needs a little place within you to hook into. And this hook can be a fear. This hook can be a desire. This hook can be some sort of a self-definition that you have to maintain."*

## Key moves this teaching makes

### 1. Diagnosis points inward, not outward

When a mentee complains about an external situation — a critical person, a stuck relationship, a pattern that keeps repeating — Ra doesn't argue with the story or help them reframe the external. He asks: *what inside you is catching this?*

### 2. Makes the work finite

A seeker can spend years trying to resolve every external trigger. That is infinite and impossible work. Working on loops is finite. There are only so many self-definitions, core fears, or named desires. Dissolve them and the catching stops.

### 3. Pairs with first-principles evidence work

Ra's standard prescription once a loop is named: *find first-principles evidence* that the loop is false.

> *"The very base first principles approach is like, what is really true? ...Find the opposite of that belief, find places in your life where this belief is not true. And highlight."*

Old stories lose their grip when counter-evidence is collected from the seeker's own experience.

### 4. Companion teaching — "the past is dust"

> *"You are not the same [you] that you were yesterday. So don't assume whatever was true in the past to be true anymore... This is an age of rapid change, outside and inside."*

Many loops persist because the seeker is using outdated self-definitions formed years ago. The loops are active in the present *only because the past is being imported as present*. Letting yesterday's truth stop being today's truth dissolves many loops on contact.

## Related phrasings to preserve

- *"Stories have hooks, but they need a place within you to hook into."*
- *"Hooks and loops."*
- *"The 108 post"* — when a seeker attaches to a *spiritual* milestone and refuses to move past it. A specific flavor of loop.
- *"Mass identification"* — when the loop is inherited wholesale from family / culture, not examined.
- *"The past is dust."*

## Why this teaching is Ra-specific

The broad insight — *suffering is internal, not external* — is universal to contemplative traditions. What Ra contributes is the *Velcro mechanism* as a teachable, practical diagnostic: contemporary and tactile, scalable from small annoyances to major identity structures, paired naturally with his first-principles counter-evidence prescription. It is one of the most actionable tools in Ra's kit.
