## Which Ramana teaching this is

The Tamil phrase **Summa Iru** (also rendered *Chumma Iru*) translates as **"Simply Be"** or **"Just Be"** or **"Be As You Are."** Ra frames it as Ramana Maharshi's deepest teaching — distinct from, and prior to, the more famous "Who Am I?" self-inquiry. Source: 33-minute solo YouTube teaching, 2025-03-02 ("Ramana Maharshi's Simplest, Deepest Teaching for Enlightenment").

Ra's framing:

> *"Today we are going to explore together the deepest teaching of Ramana Maharshi which in Tamil is Chumma Iru and the English translation of that is simply be or just be or be as you are."*

"Who Am I?" is an active inquiry practice. Summa Iru is the prior or residual state the inquiry points toward — the effortless presence that remains when all becoming, doing, and grasping has been set aside.

## Two-word etymology

### Summa / Chumma — "simply" with playfulness

Two layers Ra emphasizes:

1. **Radical simplification.** *"Removing all the complexity and making it just so simple."*
2. **Playfulness.** *Chumma* carries a colloquial, playful connotation. Ra links this directly to **Leela** (Sanskrit: divine play). Life — including the dark nights — is the play of God.

> *"Life is a play of God. Take away the seriousness, take away the complexity... And what remains is this freedom."*

### Iru — "be" (not "become")

Ordinary consciousness is always **becoming** — identifying with a story, a role, a plan, an anxiety. Iru is the invitation to stop becoming and simply **be** — which is not an action but the cessation of grasping.

> *"We are normally becoming. The ego identifies with a person, a concept, an idea, a story of who you are."*

## The preparation: removing what is not you

Ra connects Summa Iru to **Neti Neti** ("not this / not this") — the Advaita practice of systematically rejecting everything that is not the essential self. The preparation phase (removing, letting go, letting go) is the labor. Simply Being is the result.

## Ahamkara and emptiness

Ra names **Ahamkara** (the "I-maker") — the faculty that constantly constructs a doer-identity — and connects it to **Shunyata** (emptiness):

> *"When you see the hollowness, when you see the illusionary nature of I, then you are not dependent on that anymore. You don't need that foundational story to exist."*

## The no-doer pointing

Ra quotes the Bhagavad Gita — *tena vina trinamapi na calati* (*"without the will of God, not even a blade of grass moves"*):

> *"Even though it looks like somebody else is talking with you, isn't this a play? Everyone is dancing to the music that they don't even know that they're hearing."*

## Key pointings (verbatim)

- *"Take away the seriousness, take away the complexity... And what remains is this freedom."*
- *"All you have to do is let go, let go, let go."*
- *"You can meditate for 30 years in a cave, in a monastery — in this moment right now, you can let go."*
- *"There is nothing else. Nobody else, no troubles, no you, no God, no separation. Only God, only you — whichever you say, it's the same thing."*
- *"Breathe, notice, let go."* — Ra's compressed three-word practice pointing to the same essence.

## Personal relationship

Ra has said Summa Iru was pivotal on his own path but didn't come easily — the mind kept trying to conceptualize and grasp it. He studied at Ramana's ashram in Tiruvannamalai; the Ramana connection is lineage-embedded, not only intellectual.
