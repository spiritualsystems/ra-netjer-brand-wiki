## The practice in one line

**B-N-L: Breathe. Notice. Let go.** Ra's compressed three-word daily practice for returning to awareness at any moment — in any situation, with any emotional trigger, on any schedule. Primary public source: 2025-01-07 YouTube teaching (23m52s), "The Simplest Most Profound Technique for Spiritual Awakening" — the channel's top-viewed teaching video.

## Why "technique" in air quotes

Ra opens the video by qualifying the word:

> *"I don't even know if you can really call it a technique. Once you go deep enough into understanding what this is, you will discover that this is actually your true nature in itself."*

BNL is not a practice *added on top of* who you are — it is a remembering of who you already are.

## Mnemonic: Big Nose Llama

Ra names his memory device explicitly: **BNL = Big Nose Llama**. A deliberately silly mnemonic that makes the three letters stick.

## The three letters unpacked

### B — Breathe

> *"Breathing is such an important thing, a profound thing, because it is what connects this body to spirit."*

- Grounds awareness into the body, which is always anchored in the present moment
- Makes it hard for the mind to run into the past or the imagined future
- Etymologically, *spirit* comes from *breath* — grounding the step in lineage (pranayama)

### N — Notice

> *"I say notice very particularly because you are noticing what is here... It's not even an action. To notice is to bring awareness. And you are awareness."*

Telling the seeker to notice subtly reminds them that they *are* the noticer, not the noticed. This is self-inquiry compressed into a one-word instruction.

### L — Let go

> *"This is not an action. This is an un-action... or a de-action, which is simply stopping that holding on."*

The ego grasps constantly — both through desire and aversion. Let go is neither action nor inaction: it is the suspension of the grasping mechanism.

## How the three steps form a natural series

1. **Breathe** → expands consciousness from the contracted ego-grip
2. **Notice** → from that expanded state, the grasping is immediately apparent
3. **Let go** → from that recognition, the letting-go is obvious

> *"It becomes really natural for me to let go. So it's a natural series of things."*

This is why Ra frames it as "not really a technique" — the steps don't require effort so much as permission.

## How to deploy it

Ra's instruction: build BNL into a trigger.

> *"Make it a trigger. Make it a trigger which starts with breathing."*

Valid trigger conditions:
- Fear, anger, stress of any kind
- Captured by the ego mind
- Difficulty falling asleep
- Any ordinary activity: walking, talking, eating

> *"Whether you're just walking, you're talking, you're eating, you're about to fall asleep — you can still practice this."*

The practice does not require a cushion, a session, or a dedicated window. It lives inside ordinary moments — its distinguishing feature as a *daily* practice.

## Relationship to Summa Iru

BNL is not a lesser version of Summa Iru — it is the approach route for practitioners whose minds need scaffolding to arrive at effortless being. The destination is identical.

> *"My approach to Just Be, to make it more practical for myself... three steps and three words... gives you the structure, the deeper understanding."*

## What sustained practice reveals

> *"This can just remove that layer which you're holding on to, that filter upon your consciousness — it can clear a way to reveal what is already here, which is enlightenment, which is the light of who you are."*

BNL does not *produce* enlightenment — it *removes the obstruction* to enlightenment that is already present.
