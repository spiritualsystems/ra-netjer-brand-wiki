## The teaching

Two interlocking teachings: why the **teacher-student relationship** has a specific architecture, and why **etiquette** inside that architecture is load-bearing, not optional.

## 1. The Bridge

> *"The teacher is a bridge between the physical, the human, and the divine. And then my connection with you — I use that connection as a bridge to bring the light through that connection to you."*

The teacher is not the destination. The teacher is not the source. The teacher is the **connective structure** that allows divine light to reach a specific student through a specific channel.

Implications:

- **Gaps in the bridge = gaps in transmission.** If the student is half-attending, split across multiple teachers, or inconsistently committed, the bridge is porous. Light can't land fully.
- **The teacher is load-bearing, not replaceable.** A *living* bridge is Ra's requirement. Practices with deceased-only teachers are respected as teachings but, in Ra's framing, don't take a seeker all the way to enlightenment without a living bridge.
- **The student is also load-bearing.** The bridge works both ways. The student's commitment, presence, and openness are what make the bridge structurally sound from their end.
- **The bridge evolves with the teacher.** When Ra integrates new material from his own guru, the bridge shifts. Students who can't re-anchor to the shifted bridge may fall off. This is considered normal, not a failure.

## 2. The Etiquette

> *"Etiquette is important for enlightenment. If you don't follow etiquette, you're not going to reach that — at least not in its fullness."*

### The four etiquettes

1. **Full attendance at live sessions.** Camera on. Present. Not half-listening while doing email. Show up the way you would for surgery on your own body — because in a real sense, that's what a transmission session is.

2. **No cross-lineage transmission to other students.** If you (as a student) are attending other teachers' work, that's your prerogative. But do not introduce Ra's other students to those teachers without Ra's blessing. The reason is architectural, not political: each student's bridge is calibrated. Introducing another transmission into someone else's bridge destabilizes it.

3. **Commitment to the full container.** The six-month Integrated Awakening Program commitment is not a pricing consideration — it's the minimum depth the work requires. *"If you don't do it fully, it doesn't work fully."*

4. **Openness to the teacher's evolution.** Ra expects to keep deepening under his own gurus. As he integrates their material, his transmission shifts. Students are invited to walk with the evolution — and if they can't, to choose another pathway (Ra will even recommend one).

### The failure mode — mixed transmission

Ra is explicit that one form of disrepair is specifically dangerous: **attending another teacher whose transmission carries patterns incompatible with the lineage you are currently receiving.**

If you receive a transmission whose energetic signature is *"leaving the teacher,"* and you then try to anchor into a teacher-student bridge, the bridge will keep dissolving. The two patterns are mechanically incompatible. **Transmissions are not neutral.** What you receive, you carry.

## Why this teaching is structurally Ra-specific

Most non-dual teachers frame teacher-student relationships loosely: *follow what resonates, find your own teacher, the inner guru is the real teacher.* Ra's framing is unusual in the contemporary field:

- He insists the outer teacher is **necessary** (not merely helpful) for most seekers
- He insists on **commitment** inside the container (not "come when you feel like it")
- He treats **etiquette as metaphysical**, not social
