## The teaching

Ra has a named practice: **Sacred Silliness**. The instruction is to find a way to look at whatever mental content is capturing you — however heavy, compulsive, or serious it seems — from a perspective where it is *silly*. Laughter is the mechanism that breaks the hold.

> *"As soon as you laugh at something, you take its power away. You make it so light. Even when it's something so heavy, you come back to love when you laugh. You can come back to your natural state."*

This is not dismissal of the problem. It is a **perspective shift** that disarms the bound-perspective before returning to address the content from a free one. Sourced from Ra's 2025-04-19 public YouTube teaching ("Stop Negative Intrusive Thoughts Easily & Joyfully").

## Origin: the Bangalore full moon

Ra traces this practice to a personal moment — a day spent entirely in hopelessness, meaninglessness, and suicidal ideation. His brother invited him outside to see the full moon. The sight broke through all the momentum:

> *"Looking at the full moon just made me laugh out loud. And I just laughed and laughed and laughed at the full moon."*

The laughter came from a sudden recognition of freedom — the mind's heaviness had been total, then completely dissolved by one shift in attention. Ra then extended this to an intentional practice: *what if I could do this deliberately, not just when the moon surprises me?*

## The moon-mind symbol

> *"The moon is the symbol for the mind, because it is the reflection of the light of the sun — and the sun is our true awareness being reflected back to us as our mind."*

## How laughter works — the multi-perspective mechanism

1. **Stuck/looping thoughts bind awareness** because you are inside one perspective — the perspective of the aspect attached to the pattern. From *that* perspective, the problem feels total.
2. **Laughter forces a perspective shift.** To laugh at something requires looking at it from *above* that perspective — which means you've already stepped outside the bound state.
3. **From the new vantage point**, the problem is now resolvable, reframable, or simply light enough to release.
4. **Then you can return** to other perspectives — compassion, problem-solving, skillful action — from a place of freedom rather than compulsion.

> *"Once you are able to be free of it, to see it from one perspective of laughter and silliness, then you can shift your perspective. Oh, let me look at it from a different way. From a problem-solving perspective, from a perspective of compassion."*

## The joke

Ra closes the teaching with a joke he says he loves:

> *"Why are angels able to fly? Because they take themselves lightly."*

Characteristic — Ra uses the joke to *demonstrate* the principle rather than just state it.

## What this is not

- Not instruction to bypass, suppress, or ignore problems
- Not mockery or dismissal of others' suffering
- Not a replacement for medical or psychological help when needed

## Key phrases

- *"Sacred Silliness"* — Ra's own name for this practice
- *"As soon as you laugh at something, you take its power away."*
- *"There's nothing so serious that it cannot be seen as silly."*
- *"Why are angels able to fly? Because they take themselves lightly."*
- *"You come back to love when you laugh."*
