## The teaching

Enlightenment experiences should not be rare peak events — they should become the baseline of daily life. Ra's explicit framing from the 2026-01-05 transmission satsang:

> *"I want enlightenment to be the new normal in your life."*

## The argument

1. In the modern information age, people are bombarded constantly with experiences. The exceptional dissolves quickly into the noise.
2. A single powerful enlightenment experience is therefore insufficient — it needs to be reinforced repeatedly until it stops feeling exotic.
3. The community structure (multiple weekly sessions, sharing circles, daily chat interaction) exists specifically to create this repeated exposure.
4. The goal: *interacting with your own light* becomes as unremarkable — and as essential — as eating or sleeping.

## Why this is pedagogically significant

This teaching inverts the typical spiritual-culture stance that treats peak experiences as inherently rare and precious. Ra treats them as reproducible training events.

The aspiration is not to collect rare enlightenment experiences but to develop such familiarity with these states that they cease to be *experiences at all* and become the new frame through which ordinary life is perceived.

This pairs with the [10,000 Dimensions](ten-thousand-dimensions.md) teaching: if there are 10,000 distinct dimensions of your nature to access, you need more than one lifetime of rare-peak-experience pacing to cover them all. Weekly repeated contact is the only pragmatic approach.

## Community structure as pedagogical infrastructure

The normalcy-aspiration explains the frequency of offerings: multiple sessions per week, ongoing community chat, sharing circles. *You cannot make something normal by doing it once a month.*

## Relationship to the Black Belt Framework

The [Black Belt Framework](black-belt-framework.md) maps degrees of realization. The "new normal" teaching is the *attitudinal precondition* for moving through those degrees: you cannot accelerate your progression if you treat each experience as too precious to repeat, too sacred to examine, too rare to expect again. Regular repetition is the mechanism; normalcy is both the method and a partial measure of progress.
