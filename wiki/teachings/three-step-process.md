## The teaching

A replicable three-step framework Ra teaches for transmuting pain into fuel for enlightenment.

The frame: *suffering is not something that happens to you; it is creation by your higher self to awaken you.* The three steps are the technology for realizing this in real time.

## The three steps

### Step 1 — Oneness with the Creator

Recognize that the situation is not external imposition but **your own multidimensional creation**:

> *"You are a multidimensional creator."*
>
> *"Aham Brahmaasmi. I and the Brahma are one."*

This is not a claim of blame. It is a reclaiming of the creator-identity that victimhood foreclosed. The ordinary "I" is the character; the higher-self "I" is the screenwriter. Both are you.

### Step 2 — Release the Duality / the Stories

> *"Don't fall into the story of it."*
>
> *"Hell is believing fully the stories of the ego."*
>
> *"Take your power back from these stories. You have given power to these stories that reinforce your victimhood."*

Every story has a shape: victim / villain / rescuer / betrayed / abandoned / failed. The human perspective loves the story because it creates coherence. The story is what keeps you inside the pain. Releasing the story does not deny what happened — it withdraws the *meaning* the ego attached.

A physical practice Ra teaches alongside this step: **"take your power back"** as an embodied gesture — hands pulling inward toward the heart / navel — while releasing the stories. Movement makes the release real in the nervous system.

### Step 3 — See Enlightenment in Everything (through LFG)

> *"Three, see enlightenment in everything... see enlightenment even in what looks like hell."*

The perceptual shift happens through three lenses, in sequence:

- **L — Love.** Everything that ever happened to you happened out of love. *"There is no other reason for anything else to happen. It was done with the deepest love, every deed."*
- **F — Forgiveness.** Let go of the holding, unconditionally. Forgiveness here is not moral exoneration of others; it is releasing the *internal weight* the grievance required you to carry.
- **G — Gratitude.** See the event as a blessing. The suffering was the doorway to exactly this growth.

Ra's shorthand: **LFG**.

## Supporting teachings Ra folds into the frame

### Heart softening, not heart breaking

> *"When your heart breaks, it doesn't actually break and shatter. It just becomes softer... instead of heartbreak, you should call it heart softening."*

### Contraction vs. expansion as response choice

> *"Every major event in your life, you have the choice. Do you contract or do you expand?"*

### Everything has happened for you, by your design

> *"Everything that has happened to you, has happened for you, by your design."*

The distinction between "for you" (benefit) and "to you" (imposition) is load-bearing. The same events become either trauma or initiation depending on which preposition you hold.

### Maya — the playful creation

> *"This is the playful creation. This is Maya. This is the playful creation of your higher self for you."*

## Lineage context

Ra cites **Lojong** (Tibetan Buddhist mind-training tradition) as the source tradition of the phrase *"driving all obstacles into the pathway of awakening."* He does not claim the Three-Step Process as an invention; he names it as his articulation of an ancient practice.

He also paraphrases **Ralph Waldo Emerson**: *"In each and every tragedy is hidden the seed of an equal or greater fortune."*

## Where this teaching lives in Ra's own life

Ra's **Mangalore period** (see [../identity/origin-story.md](../identity/origin-story.md)) — his mother hospitalized, a deep betrayal, days of slow walking meditation — is his own forging of this framework. He did not theorize it; he lived it first and then named it.
