## The teaching in brief

Enlightenment does not require perfection. The idea that you must reach some perfect standard before you deserve enlightenment is a self-judgment — and that self-judgment is precisely what creates the felt sense of separation from the divine. Imperfection, received with humility and self-compassion, is itself the opening.

Source: 30-minute public YouTube teaching, 2025-01-16, "Your 'Imperfection' is the KEY to Enlightenment."

## The core definition

> *"Enlightenment is the acceptance of your divinity while being imperfect. It is the humility to recognize your imperfection and not push yourself to be perfect in any way."*

Ra's most direct counter to spiritual-perfectionism — the belief that enlightenment belongs only to those who have cleaned themselves up, resolved all their issues, or achieved sustained equanimity. The recognition of imperfection, done with genuine humility, is the *doorway*.

## The divinity-untouched principle

> *"Even if you have committed the greatest sin, even if you judge yourself to be the worst, even if you feel completely unworthy — your divinity is still untouched by any of that, untainted by any of that."*

The divine nature cannot be stained by actions taken within duality. This is not moral relativism — Ra is addressing seekers locked in self-punishment loops, not licensing harmful behavior. The logic is cosmological: the infinite cannot be diminished by the finite experiences of its temporary expression.

## Ego perfection vs. humility

**Ego perfection** — inability to receive feedback, need to defend one's worldview against all challenge:

> *"Our ego just continues to try to protect itself, even saying the worst mistakes are justified."*

**Humility** — not smallness, but accurate seeing:

> *"Humility is really the ability to see the full picture. The ability to see your divinity and at the same time your humanity."*

## The caretaker personality

> *"The ego identity self is then replaced by what is known as a caretaker personality, which is an ever-evolving, free and open and humble way of looking at things."*

Not no-self (you still need to show your name to the DMV), but a loosely held, functional identity that knows itself as provisional:

> *"You don't fully believe [your identity]. Just enough to function in this world."*

## The self-judgment / separation link

> *"This is your self-judgment that makes you feel separate."*

Seekers who feel spiritually distant from God or their own nature are often not distant because of their sins or imperfections — they are distant because of the *judgment* about the imperfections. The separation is generated by the judgment, not by the behavior.

## Self-forgiveness

> *"That is why God can forgive you no matter what. Give that to yourself. You don't need to wait for God to give it to you. Because you are God."*

## Wabi-sabi as closing image

> *"The Japanese concept of wabi-sabi — putting together a broken cup, lining it with gold, holding it together to show the beauty in that imperfection. That is wise."*

Imperfection made visible and even honored, rather than hidden or corrected. The gold lining does not cover the cracks — it shows them with care. The breaks become the beauty.

## The permission grant

> *"Let this give you permission — if you needed it — to accept your enlightenment. Even in the presence of all this imperfection. To see the light even in these shadows."*
