## The teaching

> *"A correction is a strong blessing. When you surrender to that correction, when you receive the blow of that weapon on the ego, willingly, lovingly, gratefully... this is spiritual maturity — to receive a correction and recognize it as a blessing."*

Correction — a teacher, life, God, or one's own guru pointing out what is off — is not punishment. It is a high-intensity transmission specifically aimed at the ego's holdings. The ego experiences it as wrathful; the deeper self experiences it as love, delivered precisely.

## The four channels of correction

Correction can arrive through any of four channels, all legitimate, all to be received with gratitude. Ra's four-way invocation:

> *"Durga, Amba, Mother, thank you for this correction. Teacher, thank you for this correction. Life, thank you for this correction. God, thank you for this correction. You have brought me closer to enlightenment."*

1. **Guru / teacher correction** — direct, sometimes public or private.
2. **Life correction** — circumstances configured to reveal the holding. Illness, loss, betrayal, or collapse can function as correction when received well.
3. **Inner-teacher correction** — the conscience / higher self speaking. The quiet internal *"that was off"* that matures into *"that can change."*
4. **God / Divine Mother correction** — the impersonal-yet-personal correction of the field itself. Durga / Kali energy is the mythological form: *"the weapon of mass destruction for the ego."*

## Why correction is felt as wrathful

Ra names **Durga / Kali** as the mythic archetype:

> *"Like when you take the sunlight, which is already so bright, cannot look at the sun, the darkness just disappears, but then, you hold a magnifying glass to the sun, that is Kali, that is Durga."*

The Divine Feminine's wrathful aspect is sunlight-through-a-magnifying-glass: the same light, focused with intensity, burning what cannot remain. Resistance to correction = resistance to the light's focus on one's own holdings. Surrender to correction = letting the light do its work.

## Lineage-inherited pattern

Ra's receptivity to correction is lineage-rooted. He has received rare, wrathful corrections from his second guru (Ahjan), and continues to receive corrections from his current guru (Sai Maa). He frames this continuity as the pattern he models for his own students: receive it, don't defend it, celebrate it.

When Ra himself corrects a student, the form is typically gentle — but the stance expected is the same. See [the-bridge-and-the-etiquette.md](the-bridge-and-the-etiquette.md).

## What spiritual maturity sounds like

Ra models the receiving-voice:

> *"Durga, Amba, Mother, thank you for this correction. Teacher, thank you for this correction. Life, thank you for this correction. God, thank you for this correction."*

This is reusable language. It is the opposite of the ego's protest-voice (*"but I didn't mean..."* / *"they don't understand..."* / *"it's not fair that..."*). Spiritual maturity replaces protest with gratitude, without requiring that the intensity of the correction be diminished.
