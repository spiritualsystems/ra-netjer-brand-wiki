## The teaching in one line

> *"Places of power are not for us to put power outside, but for us to remind ourselves of our power within."*

Places of power and times of power **amplify what is already within you**. Pilgrimage, sacred geography, equinoxes, birthdays, full-lineage holy days — all of them are collaboration sites with the field, not sources of power external to you.

## Core principle

The common spiritual error is to treat a holy site as a *source* of spiritual power, and to treat oneself as a depleted or incomplete vessel needing to "get" that power from somewhere else. Ra inverts it:

- You are the power
- The place reveals, focuses, amplifies what is already in you
- The right relationship to the place is *reminder*, not *acquisition*

Putting power outside creates dependency on pilgrimage. Keeping power within allows pilgrimage to deepen what you already carry.

## Places of power Ra references in his own practice

- **Sri Aurobindo's Auroville, South India** — where Ra's pre-guru healing occurred
- **Tiruvannamalai, India** — Ramana Maharshi's mountain; site of Ra's first-guru studentship
- **Mangalore, South India** — site of Ra's Dark Night walking meditation
- **Mount Kurama, Kyoto, Japan** — where Ahjan was healed from cancer; Kifune Shrine sits on this mountain, near where Ra spent 2.5 months
- **Mount Shasta, California** — where Ra received his Vajrayana Buddhist ordination in heavy snow, practicing tummo in a t-shirt and jeans
- **Shirdi Sai Baba temples** — root-lineage-guru sites for Ra's current guru Sai Maa
- **Torrey Pines Reserve, San Diego** — the enlightenment site of Ahjan's own root guru
- **Place of Refuge (Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau), Hawaii Big Island** — a gate-marking site in Ra's own path
- **Mauna Kea, Hawaii Big Island** — high-altitude volcanic site used in retreat practice

### Pattern: places-of-power match the stage of the path

The places that have served Ra at each phase reflect what that phase required. Auroville for release. Mangalore for embodied integration. Mount Shasta for ordination. Kyoto for lineage connection. Hawaii for deepening. The biography reads as a geographically-placed sequence of practices.

## Times of power

Time-based windows Ra explicitly names as collaborable with:

- **Spring Equinox** (around March 20–21) — a transmission center in his teaching year
- **Navaratri** — 9-day divine-feminine festival
- **Ra's birthday (August)** — anchor for the annual Hawaii Adventure Retreat
- **Ram Navami** — birth of Rama
- **Kali Yuga wrapping** — cosmic-cycle context; the age is ending; the veil is thinning

Ra's general rule:

> *"When it's a time of power, that is the time to make a push. That is the time to say yes to life. And it can change everything."*

## How this pairs with "you are the creator"

Places of power and times of power are external reminders. [You are the creator](you-are-the-creator.md) is the internal counterpart. Retreats are practical vehicles for pairing them: seekers travel to a place of power **so that they can discover the power within themselves more directly**. If the retreat is doing its work, seekers leave not only with memories of the place, but with re-ownership of their own amplifying capacity.

## Cautions

- **Don't put God outside of you.** *"Each deity there, each statue there as a reflection of your divinity. Don't put God outside of you."*
- **Handle amplified power responsibly.** Ra's three-word formula: *"Harmlessness, selflessness, impeccability."*
- **Don't fetishize the site.** The site serves you; you don't serve the site.
