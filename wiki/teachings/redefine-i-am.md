## The teaching

Suffering is not caused by external circumstances — it is caused by a **mistaken self-definition**. The seeker has inherited a limited identity: "I am this body, this mind with limiting thoughts, this personality with its preferences and story." From within that cage, suffering is inevitable because the identity is too small for what you actually are.

The solution is not therapy, positive thinking, or fixing the external. It is to **redefine "I AM"** at the level of consciousness itself. Sourced from Ra's 2025-07-06 public YouTube teaching ("The Real Cause of All Your Suffering and How to End It").

## Root cause framing

> *"The cause of your suffering is you believe you are small."*

This is Ra's most direct public statement of the root diagnosis. It bypasses the catalogue of specific sufferings (relationships, money, health, purpose) and locates all of them in one place: the wrongly inherited self-definition.

## The mechanism

Every time a belief surfaces in the shape of *"I am a victim of this,"* *"I am small,"* *"I cannot do this,"* the instruction is to interrupt it and actively replace it with the larger definition:

> *"I am that I am. I am limitless. I am the spirit expressing myself through this human personality. I am the light itself. I am free. I am unlimited. Beyond all separation. I am divinity embodied. I am an eternal being, knowing myself through this seemingly limited experience."*

This is not affirmation-as-wishful-thinking — Ra points to an *already-true* recognition. The limited identity was picked up from the world; the unlimited identity is what was there before it was covered.

## Corroboration from the 2025-10-23 public teaching

> *"Spiritual awakening is truly about discovering what you are not... the false self, the ego in general, that whole ego becomes that obstacle. It's really the only obstacle between you and the true you."*

Where "Redefine I AM" instructs the seeker to *install* the true identity, the 2025-10-23 teaching emphasizes the subtractive path: discover what you are *not* first. The two are sequential, not competing: strip, then install.

## Suffering as a "vacuum" mechanism

> *"You let go of who you are not, then you create that space, that vacuum, that clarity in which there is space for enlightenment or true realization, self-realization, God realization to happen."*

The false self must be removed to create the space that allows self-realization to enter.

## "God is goding with God"

One of Ra's most compressed cosmological statements:

> *"You are God and you are interacting with God always. Everything around you is God. So as I say, God is goding with God."*

If there is only God, there is no "other" causing your suffering — and the you that suffers is also God learning through the play.

## The "coming down the mountain" integration arc

Ra describes a multi-phase arc after initial realization:

1. Peak experiences — Samadhis, Satoris, *"heaven on Earth"*
2. Ego-dissolution — *"the absolute removal of everything I knew"*
3. Post-dissolution integration — *"I had to relearn how to walk, how to talk... how to be human. Using Google Calendar, actually planning things with others, learning to manage money"*
4. Integration: *"the world becomes your mirror. Your teacher."*

This mirrors the Zen ox-herding pictures tradition. The false self is not simply dissolved by a single realization event; integration requires re-inhabiting ordinary human life with the light turned on.

## Key phrases

- *"The cause of your suffering is you believe you are small."*
- *"Redefine I AM in your own consciousness."*
- *"Wake up and remember who you are."*
- *"I am not here to give you something new. I'm here to help you remember."*
- *"Spiritual awakening is truly about discovering what you are not."*
- *"God is goding with God."*
- *"Rejoice. It is happening for you."*
