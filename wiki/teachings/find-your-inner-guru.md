## The core teaching

The outer guru — any guru, any teacher, any master — is a **mirror**, not a source. When you see the guru clearly, you see your own light. The purpose of the outer guru is to help you discover and activate the guru **within you**.

Ra's summary, delivered as the gift he wanted to transmit at the December 2025 Sai Maa Spiritual (R)Evolution keynote (Ra was invited by Sai Maa as keynote speaker; cross-community audience of ~700 in-person plus online):

> *"I saw my teacher clearly. Very simple. Very simple. I saw my teacher as they are... When I saw my guru in his fullness, I saw my own light. In seeing my teacher's enlightenment clearly, I became enlightened."*

The guru-within and the guru-without are not opposed. The outer teacher is the mechanism through which you contact the inner teacher — until the inner teacher becomes directly accessible.

## The Swiss bank account analogy

> *"Imagine if you had $50 trillion right now. You had all the money in the world. But then you forgot you had it. You had it all locked away in a nice Swiss bank account... and you forgot the account number. You forgot the password."*

The outer guru is the "I have forgotten my password button" — the recovery mechanism for what was never lost, only inaccessible. The wealth (enlightenment, divinity) is already fully present. The teacher does not give something new; the teacher restores access.

## Why contrast exists

> *"You're born enlightened. But when you're in so much light, it's all bright and it's all wonderful... there's no contrast. There is so much light you cannot see anything. So we come into this seemingly difficult world."*

The descent into limitation is not a mistake. It is the condition for self-recognition. Ra cites Vedanta: *Brahma satya jagat mithya* — Brahman is real, the world is appearance.

## The guru as bridge

> *"The guru is the bridge between duality, limitation, suffering — from there to the unlimited. A bridge needs to have both ends. The guru has a human form, human qualities."*

The guru's humanness is not a flaw — it is a **structural requirement**. A teacher who has dissolved entirely into pure light cannot serve as a bridge; they would have no human end.

## A true guru makes you independent

> *"A true guru makes you independent. A true guru does not make you give your power to them. Is the teacher making me completely dependent on them? Or making me independent? Am I giving my power away to them? Or are they helping me realize my true power?"*

This is a diagnostic Ra offers for evaluating any teacher — including himself.

## Arunachala and Ramana as guru-without-form

From Ra's earlier 2022-12 Tiruvannamalai satsang: Ra extends the guru definition beyond living teachers to place-power, lineage photograph, and text:

> *"Sitting in front of Ramana's picture, you can feel him teaching, you can communicate with him as if he's right there in person."*

Of Ramana's small book *Who Am I?*: *"Going deep into that self-inquiry reveals the truth... it's a complete teaching. There's nothing missing."*

## Ramana's dual prescription

> *"Either inquire within and find out who you are, or surrender — either of them works. And if you do both of them together, that can also work as well... they're really the same seen from different perspectives."*

## "You cannot surrender as an action"

> *"You can only realize that surrender is the truth and control is an illusion."*

## The ego's two strategies against the teaching

1. **Over-elevating the guru to unreachable divinity.** Creates a gulf that makes enlightenment seem impossible.
2. **Using "the inner guru is real" as bypass.** *"The ego can take this beautiful truth — that the teacher is within us — and say, 'I'm going to use my free will because the teacher is within us.' Doing things that are actually not serving the greater dharma."*
